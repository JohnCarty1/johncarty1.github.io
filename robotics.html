Biomimetic Robotics

Robotics | Mechanical Design | Bio-Inspired Systems

Over 14 months, I worked on biomimetic robotics projects focused on translating biological motion into engineered mechanical systems. The work involved thinking carefully about how natural movement can be simplified, modeled, and implemented in a controllable robotic design.

Project Overview

This work explored robotic systems inspired by biological motion, with emphasis on mechanism design, system behavior, and practical engineering tradeoffs. Rather than simply imitating nature visually, the goal was to understand which features of a biological system were mechanically meaningful and how they could be recreated in a functional robotic platform.

Engineering Problem

Biomimetic robotics presents a difficult engineering challenge because biological motion is complex, adaptive, and often nonlinear, while engineered systems must be simplified into manufacturable, controllable mechanisms. A major part of the work was identifying what aspects of the biological behavior actually mattered and how to reproduce them in a way that was mechanically realistic.

My Contributions

  • Helped translate observed motion into mechanical concepts and design choices
  • Worked through constraints involving geometry, movement, and controllability
  • Participated in iterative refinement of designs based on performance and feasibility

Technical Work

The most interesting aspect of this work was the balance between biological fidelity and engineering practicality. A design that looks biologically accurate is not necessarily the one that behaves best mechanically. This required repeated tradeoff decisions involving mechanism simplicity, actuation strategy, motion range, and the ability to physically build and test the design.

Results & Iteration
