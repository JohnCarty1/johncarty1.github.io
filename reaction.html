Reaction Wheel Attitude Control

Personal Project | Spacecraft Dynamics | Controls

This is a personal project exploring spacecraft attitude control through the design of a reaction wheel system. The goal is to better understand how rotational dynamics, actuator choice, and control logic interact in a compact stabilization-oriented platform.

Project Overview

Reaction wheels are a fundamental method of spacecraft attitude control, making them an ideal project for studying rotational dynamics in a hands-on way. This project is still in progress, but the core idea is to build a system that demonstrates how internal torque generation can be used to affect orientation.

Engineering Problem

Even at a small scale, reaction wheel systems involve meaningful tradeoffs. The system must balance wheel inertia, motor capability, structural design, and controllability. The challenge is not just making a wheel spin, but designing a system where the dynamics are strong enough to demonstrate control behavior in a measurable and physically meaningful way.

Current Focus

  • Developing the mechanical layout of the reaction wheel assembly
  • Thinking through actuator and inertia tradeoffs
  • Planning the control and sensing architecture for future testing
  • Using the project as a hands-on study of spacecraft attitude dynamics

Technical Direction

The technical interest of the project lies in the relationship between angular momentum, torque, and system response. I am especially interested in how design choices such as wheel size, mass distribution, motor performance, and frame geometry affect what the system can realistically demonstrate.
