Plasma Physics Research

Summer 2026 | Experimental Plasma Systems | Research

This upcoming summer research position will focus on experimental plasma physics and the engineering of plasma-related hardware. I am especially interested in the physical constraints that shape plasma system design, including geometry, materials, thermal considerations, and electromagnetic behavior.

Research Focus

My expected work will involve understanding and contributing to the design of laboratory plasma systems, with attention to how theory, hardware constraints, and experimental requirements interact. I am particularly excited by engineering problems where physical modeling directly informs design decisions.

Why This Interests Me

Plasma systems sit at a compelling intersection of physics and engineering. They require strong physical intuition while also demanding practical design judgment under difficult operating conditions. That combination is a major reason I am drawn to the field.

Preparation

Going into this research, I am bringing experience from robotics and hardware-based engineering work, along with a strong interest in applying physical reasoning to real experimental systems. I see this project as an opportunity to deepen both my technical knowledge and my experience working on complex laboratory hardware.

What I Hope to Develop

  • Hands-on experience with experimental plasma hardware
  • A stronger understanding of design under physical and laboratory constraints
  • Greater fluency in linking theory, modeling, and system implementation

Next Steps

This page will be updated as the project develops to include the system context, my technical contributions, and the engineering challenges that arise during the work.
